## What Actually Makes Residential SOCKS5 Different (And Why It Matters)

Three proxy types dominate the market: datacenter, ISP (static residential), and rotating residential. Each has trade-offs.

Datacenter proxies are fast and cheap. They also get blocked by anti-bot systems on the regular because their IPranges are publicly documented as belonging to hosting providers. Sites like Instagram, sneaker drops, ticketing platforms — they sniff datacenter ranges and reject them on contact.

Residential proxies route through real homes. Real ISPs. Real consumer devices. That's the whole point. When a target site sees your traffic, it looks identical to any random Comcast or BT subscriber browsing on a Tuesday night.

The SOCKS5 part maters too. HTTP proxies only handle web traffic. SOCKS5 is protocol-agnostic — it caries TCP and UDP, suports authentication, and doesn't rewrite your packets the way HTTP proxies do. If you're running anything more complex than a curl command, you'll fel the difference.

> Plain language summary: Buying residential SOCKS5 proxy bandwidth gives you real-home IP addresses paired with a flexible protocol that works for almost any tool. Datacenter is cheaper but easier to block. Residential SOCKS5 is the middle-ground sweet spot for most serious use cases.

## Setup Walkthrough — Geting Webshare SOCKS5 Working in Five Minutes

Different tools, different configs. Three common scenarios:
